Meeting Subject: PhRMA/FDA/Academia Safety Graphics Working Group

Attendees

George Rochester (Chair), Matt Soukup(Co-Chair), Richard J. Anziano, Fabrice Bancken, Frank Harrell, Ken Koury, Larry Gould, Liping Huang, Qi Jiang, Robert Gordon, Susan Duke, Janelle Charles, Charles Cooper, Stephine Keeton, Leslie Kenna, Antonio Paredes, Bruce Weaver(Project Coordinator)

Absentees:

Andreas Bruckner, Navdeep Boparai, Brenda Crowe, Matthew Gribbin, Sylvia Engelen, Suzanne Demko, Federico Goodsaid

Meeting Purpose: Introduction of working group members, charter finalization, major milestones for 2010, web-hosting, and documentation.

Points of Discussion

Charter finalization
  • It was noted that the numbering in Section 1 is out of order and 1.2 is listed twice.
  • In section 1.4 should state “exploration and reporting”.
    • Perhaps these tools could identify safety problems;
      • Include exploration and reporting.
      • Better understanding in reporting.
  • Concerning the Project Scope in addition to ISS, exploratory graphs, and study reports.
    • Include that the static graphics should be printable.
  • High Level Project Plan
    • Much of what will be developed will be useful to epidemiological studies.
    • The Charter may be adapted continuously.
    • As these tools are developed they aide in the production of new drugs. Should the Scope(1.4) reflect this.
  • Milestones
    • Remove ‘Project Authority’ from the charter, leave only ‘Milestones’ as the heading for Section 2.
    • Some major safety questions must be incorporated in 2.1.a.
    • Define 2.1.a.iii “regulatory review and business processes”
      • 2.1.a.iii should be its own sub-section, 2.1.e
      • What were the “business process”?
        • This was the basis to describe how to use the pallet effectively. Recommendations for implementation of future work streams. More detail is required.
    • A discussion of 2.1.b “Develop a palette of statistical graphics for specialized reporting of clinical trials data” by Matt Soukup.
      • It is critical to development that data sets be submitted.
        • Users should be able to share how they have used graphics to display information.
        • Users should be able to submit data sets for information the do not know how to display.
        • New clinical data in CDISC format.
        • If any members of the working group have dummy data sets that could be used, they should submit them.
      • Members should discuss and share graphics they have.
        • This will require further discussion and assignments.
      • Points of discussion
  1. How will these data sets be maintained, how will users contribute to this in the future?
    • Frank Harrell will address this in the future.
  2. What will be the standards for text and graphics? e.g.
    • Resolution and properties for graphics.
    • Rules for text
    • Something beyond rules to encourage better graphics.
    • When the wiki is used how will best practices be ensured?
    • There should be some regulation of content e.g. a review process.
    • An outline to describe best practices.
    • In addition to proper credit, a disclaimer should be included that this data has not been verified for a desired level of quality.
      • Contributors should not feel responsible if their name was attached to some code and it fails to run.
      • Legal will be involved.
    • Documentation
      • A statement of purpose should be included so users know how to interpret graphs.
      • In the interest of time Section 2.4 Major Project Deliverables will be assigned to a volunteer.
        • Qi Jiang Volunteered.
  • Section 3 Project Structure
    • Frank Harrell was asked if he could recommend any others in academia with interest in the groups effort. Rich Highburger at Temple University was recommended.
    • Vendors could be brought in but not as members so the group does not loose scope.
    • Section 3.1.2(d) Patient groups and health care providers.
      • Since these graphics may end up on a label, this group is important.
      • There are no group members from section 3.1.2(d); we may include them when there is content to present for the outreach/training.
Web-hosting
  • Frank Harrell
      • Web-hosting platform can do what is required.
      • Good statistical practice is not documented. This may be a good objective.
      • Develop a compendium of test data sets with specific data sets that reflect reality.
      • Collect simulation designs;
        • 4 period cross over designs
        • Dose studies
        • 2x2 designs
      • If this could be a community platform for sharing ideas, that would be an added benefit.
      • CTSpedia just moved to Vanderbilt, and can bring various areas of expertise.
